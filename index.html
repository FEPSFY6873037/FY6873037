Abstract

The internet, one of the greatest inventions in human history. An invention allowing near instant communication between people from nearly anywhere on the planet with limited methods of regulating usage. As such this has spawned people using this service for more harm than good. One example is the computer virus. “A computer virus is a type of malicious software, or malware, that spreads between computers and causes damage to data and software. “What are Computer viruses? Definitions and types of Viruses: available at (https://www.fortinet.com/uk/resources/cyberglossary/computer-virus)(Last accessed on 1st December 2024)

This study intends on observing how computer viruses have evolved over the years in parallel with the internet, this can hopefully be used to study and predict how they will change and hopefully attempt to minimize damage future viruses could cause.

Introduction

The internet, a term originally credited to Vint Cerf, is the word used to describe the system of interconnected networks we all use in our day to day lives allowing for all of us to stay connected. The internet has grown exponentially since its inception with different pieces of software developing and changing in parallel with the internet.

This paper will look at how we can use computer viruses to study how the internet has changed through the decades starting with the creation of the ARPAnet all the way up to the modern day state of the internet and where most internet traffic is concentrated these days with demonstrations of computer viruses from the different eras of the internet.

ARPANET

Back in the 1950s, due to tensions between the USA and the Soviet Union starting to build because of differing ideologies as well as knowledge that both nations had access to weapons of mass destruction, The USA came up with a failsafe, they wanted to create a long range communication system that can be used in the event of a long range missile attack, and so, in 1965 ARPANET was born, the first ever long range network. A history of the internet | National Science and Media Museum: available at (https://www.scienceandmediamuseum.org.uk/objects-and-stories/short-history-internet) (Last accessed on 1st December 2024). ARPANET existed as a branch of the US department of defense until the invention of the world wide web in 1990, the first ever wide area network open to the entire world.

The launch of the world wide web meant dozens of individuals were able to get in contact with each other and created programs for each other, these groups all created programs that spread between one another in these groups. These were the first computer viruses distributed on the world wide web.

Most of the original computer viruses would only affect the MSDOS system, one example of this is crash.600. This virus would infect all . COM files on the Pc and will cause the screen to display random numbers and characters until the system crashes. Virus attack! The DOT-COM bubble

In the coming years personal computers would become significantly more accessible as they were smaller and so much more afforable. UK households; ownership of home computers 1985-2018: available at (https://www.statista.com/statistics/289191/household-penetration-of-home-computers-in-the-uk/) (Last accessed on 1st December 2024) This meant a lot of potential for money to be made on the internet. This resulted in a rapid increase in the stock market for any internet-based companies, incentivising investments into companies providing internet services to users. One such company that was formed during this time period was Google. Dot-Com Bubble and Bust | definition, history and facts | Britannica Money: available at (https://www.britannica.com/money/dot-com-bubble) (Accessed 1st December 2025).

During this time period internet traffic fluctuated massively, with companies being founded and going bust all of the time, where internet traffic was never wholly concentrated around three or four different domains. The Most Visited Websites Every Year Since 1995: available at (https://www.a2hosting.com/blog/the-most-visited-websites-every-year-since-1995/) (Last accessed on 1st December 2024). This stage of the internet was completely different from the one we have today, interest in creating websites was at a high point, it meant dozens of websites being published all completely different from one another, on example is DPGraph.com (http://www.dpgraph.com/) (Last accessed on 1st December 2024), a website still up today that has hardly changed since it was originally published in 2005, DPGraph 2000: Dynamic Photorealistic Graphing: archived from internet archive on 19th October 2000 (http://web.archive.org/web/20001019004433/http://www.dpgraph.com/) (Last accessed on 1st December 2024).

These turbulent times of the internet spawned one of the most well known computer viruses as a result of a university Student wanting to see the limits of computer software instead of why they were originally created, the ILOVEYOU virus. On 4th May 2000 this virus was sent over the internet and within 24 hours had infected over 45 million systems, infecting around 10% of all internet connected computers. I Love You (virus) - computer Museum of America: available at (https://www.computermuseumofamerica.org/2023/02/07/i-love-you-virus/) (Last accessed on 1st December 2024) and in the end resulting in $15 billion in damages worldwide. The history and Impact of the iloveyou virus: available at (https://www.goldskysecurity.com/the-history-and-impact-of-the-iloveyou-virus/) (Last accessed on 1st December 2024)

The Modern State OF The Internet

After the burst of the Dot-com bubble the internet calmed down significantly, a lot fewer web based companies started to get founded, but once these new companies got going they ran away, take YouTube and Facebook as examples, launched on 2005 and 2004 respectively. Ever since the burst there have been a few companies on top consitently hosting a majority of internet traffic. Top Website in the world October 2024: available at (https://www.semrush.com/website/top/) (Last accessed on 1st December 2024) Top 100 Most Visited Websites in the world April 2024: available at (https://backlinko.com/most-popular-websites) (Last accessed on 1st December 2024), both of these sources show how most of the internet traffic is concentrated around massive companies like Google YouTube or Facebook. This pattern has been consitent for years where Google and other companies have effecively developed a method to generate as much profit as effecively as possible using data harvesting.

This approach is mirrored with most modern viruses, these days you will be hard pressed to find a virus that doesn’t attempt to extract as much cash from an innocent person as possible. With WannaCry being a prolific example of a virus attempting to extract money out of innocent people with threats that they will lose all personal data unless money sent to an anonymous person over the internet. Ransomware WannaCry: All you need to know: available at (https://www.kaspersky.com/resource-center/threats/ransomware-wannacry) (Last accessed on 1st December 2024)

Example the WANNACRY virus attacking a computer: WannaCry, Petya, et al: Protecting your organisatioon from ransomware - Huntsman (https://huntsmansecurity.com/blog/wannacry-petya-et-al-protecting-your-organisation-from-ransomware/) (Last accessed on 1st December 2024)

Conclusion

This paper has discussed the evolution of computer viruses as they have evolved alongside the internet. The paper has shown that as the internet has grown, it has evolved alongside all its other non-harmful content, going from artistic to attempting to generate as much profit as possible over as short a time as possible. Mirroring how companies such as Google use the internet, granted in a less harmful method.
